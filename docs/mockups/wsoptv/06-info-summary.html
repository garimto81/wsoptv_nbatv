NEGREANU LEADS FINAL FIVE AS MAIN EVENT NEARS CONCLUSION

Day 7 - Final Table ‚Ä¢ July 16th, 2026 6:00 PM
Tournament Update

Daniel Negreanu holds a commanding chip lead as the 2026 WSOP Main Event Final Table resumes with five players remaining.

After an intense day of play that saw four eliminations, Negreanu sits with 12.4 million chips, nearly 3 million ahead of second-place Phil Ivey. The legendary duo is expected to clash in what many are calling the most anticipated heads-up battle in WSOP history.

Tom Dwan, known for his aggressive style, sits third with 8.2 million chips and has been patient, waiting for his spots. Jamie Gold, the 2006 champion, is in fourth with 5.1 million, while Phil Hellmuth - chasing his record 17th bracelet - is short-stacked with 2.4 million.

"This is what we all dream about when we enter the Main Event. Five legends battling for poker's most prestigious prize. I'm ready for whatever comes," said Negreanu during the break.

The blinds will resume at 200K/400K with a 400K big blind ante. First place pays $12.5 million while second place takes home $7.5 million.

Ivey, seeking his first Main Event bracelet, has been playing his signature patient style, picking spots carefully. His confrontation with Dwan in hand #198 - where he laid down queens to Dwan's river bluff - was the most discussed hand of the day.
