NEGREANU TAKES CHIP LEAD INTO DAY 3 OF WSOP MAIN EVENT FINAL TABLE

Wednesday, January 21st, 2026 11:20 AM
Tournament Preview

Daniel Negreanu surged into the chip lead during Day 2 action of the WSOP Main Event Final Table, setting up a thrilling Day 3 with $12.1 million on the line for first place.

With five players remaining from the original field of 8,569 entries, the $93,000,000 prize pool ensures everyone at the final table is guaranteed at least $2,000,000.

Negreanu's dominant performance saw him accumulate 12.45 million in chips, well ahead of Phil Ivey's 9.8 million. The two poker legends have traded the chip lead multiple times throughout the tournament.

"I felt really comfortable at the table today. The cards were running my way, but more importantly, I was making the right reads on my opponents." - Daniel Negreanu

Phil Ivey, a 10-time WSOP bracelet winner seeking his first Main Event title, showed his trademark patience throughout the day. His key double-up against Tom Dwan with QQ vs AK kept him in contention.

The bubble burst dramatically when Jamie Gold moved all-in with AK and ran into Negreanu's pocket aces, securing the remaining four players their minimum $2M payout.

Play resumes at 12:00 PM local time with blinds at 100,000/200,000 with a 25,000 ante. The tournament director has announced that play will continue until a winner is crowned or until 2 AM, whichever comes first.
